# Main Features of the Upgraded Version

The upgraded GEMINI-E3-EU model introduces several major developments relative to previous versions.

## Regional and Sectoral Disaggregation

The model includes an expanded geographical representation of the European Union, distinguishing key Member States individually while aggregating other countries into empirically derived regional clusters. Outside Europe, major economies such as the United States, China, and India are explicitly represented alongside aggregated world regions.

Sectoral classifications have also been refined to improve consistency with climate policy analysis and emissions accounting, with particular emphasis on energy systems, transport, manufacturing industries, and emerging low-carbon technologies.

## Emerging Energy Technologies

The model explicitly represents several technologies relevant for long-term decarbonisation pathways, including:

- Hydrogen
•	Biofuels
•	Heat-pumps
- Carbon Capture and Storage (CCS),
- Renewable electricity technologies.

These additions improve the representation of hard-to-abate sectors and allow assessment of technology-specific policy instruments and transition pathways.

## Expanded Emissions Coverage

Beyond carbon dioxide (CO₂), the model includes additional greenhouse gases such as methane (CH₄) , nitrous oxide (N₂O) and F gases, together with local air pollutants including:

- NOₓ
- SO₂
- PM2.5.

This extended coverage enables integrated assessment of climate mitigation policies and their environmental co-benefits.

## Household Heterogeneity and Labour Dynamics

The upgraded framework incorporates household heterogeneity and labour market extensions to improve analysis of distributional and employment effects associated with climate policies.

New features include:

-	Household categories differentiated by income groups
-	Skilled and unskilled labour representation
-	Imperfect labour mobility across sectors,
-	Labour reallocation mechanisms during the low-carbon transition.

## Regionalisation and Spatial Extensions

The model establishes the basis for coupling with regional information at the NUTS-2 level, enabling spatial analysis of emissions, welfare impacts, and regional policy outcomes across Europe.
