# Limitations and Future Improvements

Despite the substantial upgrades introduced under the DIAMOND project, GEMINI-E3-EU remains subject to several limitations inherent to large-scale CGE modelling frameworks.

First, the model relies on a number of behavioural and structural assumptions, including perfect competition, market-clearing conditions, and CES functional forms governing production and consumption decisions. While these assumptions provide analytical consistency and tractability, they may simplify complex real-world adjustment mechanisms and behavioural responses.

Second, the representation of technological change remains partially exogenous for several sectors and technologies. Although the model explicitly incorporates emerging technologies such as hydrogen, CCS, BECCS, biofuels, and heat pumps, technological learning, diffusion dynamics, and infrastructure constraints are not yet fully represented.

Third, the regionalisation framework at the NUTS-2 level relies on proxy-based allocation methods using employment, population, and sectoral indicators. While this approach improves spatial granularity, regional results remain dependent on the quality and availability of subnational datasets.

The labour market representation also remains relatively stylised. Although the upgraded version introduces labour heterogeneity and imperfect mobility across sectors, the current framework does not yet fully capture unemployment dynamics, skill mismatches, wage rigidities, or detailed labour market frictions.

In addition, the representation of electricity systems and energy infrastructure remains aggregated compared to dedicated bottom-up energy system models. As a result, certain operational aspects of power systems, storage technologies, grid constraints, and short-term balancing mechanisms are not explicitly modelled.

Future developments of GEMINI-E3-EU aim to further improve technological representation, labour market dynamics, spatial resolution, and integration with external modelling frameworks. Planned improvements include enhanced coupling with NUTS-2 datasets, stronger representation of infrastructure and energy system constraints, improved treatment of behavioural heterogeneity, and expanded integration with air pollution and health impact assessments.

Further work will also focus on improving model transparency, reproducibility, and accessibility through continued development of open documentation, GitHub repositories, and version-controlled releases within the DIAMOND open-science framework.
