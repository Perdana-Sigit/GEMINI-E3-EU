# CCS

In addition to conventional energy technologies, the model incorporates low-carbon and negative-emission technologies, including Carbon Capture and Storage (CCS) applied to coal, oil, and gas-fired power plants, as well as Bioenergy with Carbon Capture and Storage (BECCS). 

The inclusion of CCS technologies enables the model to analyse the role of carbon capture in decarbonising hard-to-abate sectors and reducing emissions from fossil-fuel-based electricity generation. 

CCS deployment is represented through additional capital and operational costs associated with carbon capture processes, allowing the model to evaluate the competitiveness of CCS technologies under different carbon pricing and climate policy scenarios. 

BECCS technologies additionally allow the representation of potential negative emissions pathways within long-term decarbonisation strategies.
