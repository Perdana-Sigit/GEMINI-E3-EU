# Energy Module

The energy module represents energy production, transformation, and consumption within the GEMINI-E3-EU framework. Energy sectors are integrated through nested CES structures, allowing substitution between fuels, electricity technologies, and other energy carriers in response to changes in prices, technological developments, and climate policies.

The module explicitly represents:

- Coal
- Oil
- Natural gas
- Petroleum products
- Electricity
- Hydrogen
- Biofuels

Electricity generation is modelled with detailed technological disaggregation, including fossil-fuel-based and renewable technologies such as coal, gas, oil, nuclear, hydro, solar, wind, and biofuel-based generation. The module also incorporates Carbon Capture and Storage (CCS) technologies and heat pumps introduced through the DIAMOND project upgrades.

The energy module interacts directly with the production, emissions, household consumption, and international trade modules, supporting analysis of energy transitions, decarbonisation pathways, and climate policy impacts.
