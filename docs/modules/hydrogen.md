# Hydrogen

This EU version of the model has been enhanced by the introduction of a new hydrogen sector, which focuses on producing hydrogen through water electrolysis. This innovation is based on cost structures outlined in the International Energy Agency (IEA) report titled **"The Future of Hydrogen"**. The model differentiates electricity prices by region to accurately reflect local conditions while maintaining a consistent cost structure for hydrogen production across all areas. 

The baseline scenario incorporates existing climate policies, allowing for the endogenous calibration of hydrogen penetration based on figures from the IEA's World Energy Outlook (WEO) 2022. Specifically, the model assumes hydrogen will be exclusively utilized for road transport, and the nested Constant Elasticity of Substitution (CES) functions are recalibrated annually to align with the projected hydrogen adoption rates.

Furthermore, the integration of hydrogen into the GEMINI-E3 model facilitates a more nuanced understanding of the energy landscape, particularly in the context of decarbonization efforts. This development addresses the need for improved representation of energy inputs, which includes biofuels and hydrogen, while reflecting the deep decarbonization scenarios. 

The restructured CES nesting for both production and consumption aims to optimize energy use in various applications, including transportation and industrial processes. By accommodating these new energy inputs, the model is better equipped to analyze the economic impacts and potential of hydrogen as a critical player in achieving sustainability goals.
